It started like any other evening. Sarah came back to her dorm, balancing a stack of textbooks in one hand and her phone in the other. She fumbled with her keys, pushing open the door to the small room she shared with Lily, her cheerful, always-chatty roommate. But tonight was different. The room was silent. Eerily so. “Lily?” Sarah called out, setting her books on the desk. No response. The usual hum of Lily’s favorite playlist was missing, and the bed that was always strewn with blankets and clothes looked untouched. It was pristine. Sarah assumed Lily had gone to the library, but something gnawed at her. She couldn’t shake the feeling that something was off.

Panic set in. Sarah texted Lily. “Where are you? Did you move out?” But her phone showed only a single checkmark. Unread. She called, but the line went straight to voicemail. Sarah paced the room, every corner now devoid of any trace of her roommate. She ran to the RA’s office, her words stumbling over themselves. “My roommate... she’s gone! Everything’s gone! It’s like she never lived there!” The RA frowned. “Roommate? Sarah, you’ve always had a single room.” “What? No, that’s not right. Lily—Lily Daniels! She’s been my roommate since the start of the semester!” The RA pulled out a folder, flipping through papers. “Sarah, we don’t have a Lily Daniels in the system. You were assigned to this room alone.
